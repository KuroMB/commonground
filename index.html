Our Philosophy

People before
square footage.

Our approach is rooted in Ray Oldenburg and the Project for Public Spaces — great places are made through community participation, not top-down design. We add a practical layer: the economics of family entertainment and the behavioral psychology of parents with young children.

We don't sell renderings. We help clients understand human behavior, test ideas cheaply, and build momentum through small wins before committing large capital.

We have a deliberate bias toward existing spaces. The infrastructure is built. The parking is paid for. The community already knows where it is. Starting with what's there is more sustainable and more honest than proposing something new from scratch.

  • Rooted in Third Place Theory and the 11 Principles of Placemaking
  • Informed by motivational interviewing and behavioral change frameworks
  • Guided by "Lighter, Quicker, Cheaper" — test before you build
  • Focused on existing spaces and infrastructure whenever possible
  • Focused on the family demographic that every other redevelopment strategy ignores
